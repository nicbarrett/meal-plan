  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 3 cups white rice (dry)
  • 2 cans black beans
  • 2 cups frozen corn
  • Fresh cilantro

  • 2 red onions

No-Bake Protein Balls

Based on Nourished by Nic

‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes üçΩÔ∏è 20 balls üí™ 3-4g protein/ball

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pitted dates (10-12 Medjool)
  • 1 cup almond butter
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder (optional)
  • 1/3 cup mini chocolate chips (optional)
  • 2-3 tbsp water if needed
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. If dates are hard, soak in warm water for 5 minutes, then drain
  2. Add dates to food processor, pulse until sticky paste forms
  3. Add almond butter, protein powder, cocoa powder, and salt. Pulse until combined
  4. If too dry, add water 1 tablespoon at a time
  5. Fold in chocolate chips if using
  6. Roll mixture into 1-inch balls
  7. Place on parchment-lined tray and refrigerate 30 minutes to firm

Creamy Turkey Sausage Protein Pasta

Adapted from Jar of Lemons and Eat the Gains

‚è±Ô∏è 35 minutes üçΩÔ∏è 10 servings üí™ 35-40g protein/serving

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chickpea or lentil pasta
  • 1.5 lbs turkey Italian sausage
  • 3 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 jar sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • ¬Ω cup milk
  • 1 cup parmesan cheese
  • 24 oz marinara sauce
  • Red pepper flakes (for Nic)

Instructions:

  1. Cook protein pasta according to package directions, drain and set aside
  2. Brown turkey sausage in large skillet, breaking into bite-sized pieces

  5. Toss in cooked pasta and sun-dried tomatoes

  7. Portion into containers
  8. Nic adds red pepper flakes when reheating his portions
